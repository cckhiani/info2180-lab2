How I even Got Here...lol

Published on October 3, 2024 by Chanchal Khiani

Was it my lifelong dream to be a software developer? Let's see, when I was five I wanted to be a doctor. At nine, I was certain I would become a lawyer. Once again, at fourteen I was choosing CSEC science subjects in preparation to get into med school so I could be a doctor. The Indian genes almost won, well, I mean they kinda won regardless. So, when the time actually came for me to choose what I want to study in university, why did I pick Computer Science? If I'm being honest, at eighteen years old, I wasn't completely sure what I wanted to commit to doing for the rest of my life. This was an extremely important decision that would shape the trajectory of my entire life and it was all in MY HANDS?! This was incredibly intimidating, to say the least. I therefore did what any sensible person would do; I googled "highest paying careers" and saw Software Development at the top of the list.

BINGO! That's how eighteen year old me decided to study Computer Science. Let's just say my decision making skills have improved a bit since then. Am I mad at that decison, though? That answer has changed over the course of my time studying for this degree. In first year, I was having a really hard time keeping up and staying interested. I had no prior experience with any coding languages and felt a high sense of imposter syndrome. I even considered dropping out. However, fast forward to like two years later and I'm glad I stuck it out. With determination, consistency and practice, everything became a bit easier and began to feel more natural. I found an appreciation for what I'm learning and I can truly see myself applying this knowledge in a career that will fulfill me for my entire life. I've developed a significant interest in Web Development and I'm even considering specializing in it. I think it's safe to say that I made the right decision. Thanks Google!
